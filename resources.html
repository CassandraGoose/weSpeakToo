Washington, DC


New to Speaking or Want to Learn More?

First let's dispel some common myths.

  • You don't need to be an expert to speak on a subject. In fact, sometimes it's the best way to learn something new! You might not even have found your footing yet. One recent tech meetup was a fresh-out-of-undergrad man giving a talk on finding a job and his way in the industry.
  • There is no baseline for a talk to be "techy" enough. Don't think your talk or knowledge is "techy" enough? Nonsense! Beginner talks are often welcome, and once again you don't need to be an expert to speak on a subject. There are also many, many backgrounds fit for a good data or tech talk - project management, media, design, and more.
  • Public speaking isn't as scary as you think, and you might even like it. I promise! Practice can prove that. Start where you like. Giving short demos at meetups like Hack and Tell can be a good first step. If you know Python, even at a beginner level, consider TAing or teaching a HearMeCode class. It defnitely won't be a nightmare. You definitely won't be naked.
